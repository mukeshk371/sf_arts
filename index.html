Yosvany Terry

Festivals
July 28

The bracing Cuban saxophonist and percussionist leads his New York-based Afro-Cuban Sextet, whose members include younger brother Yunior, on bass, Oakland-born trumpeter Ambrose Akinmusire and the crackling drummer Obed Calvaire from the SFJAZZ Collective.

O. Kwon + Esmé Weijun Wang

Literary Arts
July 13

O. Kwon launches her much-anticipated debut novel, “The Incendiaries,” with a reading and conversation with Esmé Weijun Wang (“The Border of Paradise”). “The Incendiaries” follows a Korean American woman at an elite American university as she is drawn into acts of domestic terrorism by a cult tied to North Korea, and the man whose love for her mirrors the obsession that’s consuming her.

San Francisco Ballet

Festivals
July 29

San Francisco Ballet enthusiasts have two opportunities in July to see the world-class arts institution in local performance: first, at Festival Napa Valley, and second, at an afternoon concert at the Stern Grove Festival. Picnic al fresco in the Grove while enjoying a program of contemporary and classical ballet composed by historic choreographers and today's iconic dancemakers.

Common/San Francisco Symphony

Music
Aug. 1

Common, the charismatic actor, rapper and poet who won a 2015 Oscar for the song "Glory, " which he wrote with John Legend for "Selma, " joins the orchestra under Steven Reineke's baton to perform that and other hits plus pieces from his 2016 album "Black America Again. "
